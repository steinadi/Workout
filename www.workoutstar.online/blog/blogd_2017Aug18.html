WHAT TO EAT PRE AND POST WORKOUT

Posted by Luisa Onnebrink


Pre: Water with Lemon
This is perfect to get you hydrated before a sweaty workout session. And the lemon is a great detox that will clean your body and will get you ready to take on the day fully energized!

Pre: Whole Wheat Toast with Sliced Banana
When you’re getting ready for workout, carbs are your best friend! It is important to mix complex and simple carbs, so that the release of energy duing your workout is slow and steady. A whole-wheat toast or rice waffle with fruits will give you both type of carbs. The complex carbs keep you going longer during your routine, whereas the bananas help you raise your potassium levels which sink when you sweat a lot. And it is also very easy to digest. Maybe even add some creamy peanut butter for an extra energy boost!

Pre: Smoothies
If you are not super hungry before your workout, go for a smoothie. This little snack will save you a lot of time. Just put some of your favorite fruits, a cup of yoghurt and some granola or oats in a blender. Make sure you don’t exceed 10 to 20 grams of protein in your pre-workout smoothie!

Pre: Apple Wedged with Nut Butter
No, don’t go for the delicious but veeeery sugary candy bar before your workout session. Go for the apple! This way you’ll avoid a sugar crash, while fueling your body with great vitamins, minerals and antioxidants. If you’re hungry, put some nut butter on the slices. This will keep you going during your routine!

Pre: Oatmeal with fresh fruit
Oatmeal is great because it releases the sugar only very slowly into your system. This keeps your energy levels up much longer. Maybe add some fruit to your bowl and increase the fluid content of your pre-workout snack. Don’t forget to stay hydrated!

Post: Salmon with Sweet Potato
Salmon isn not just a great source of protein but it also has bioactive peptides, small protein molecules that reduce the risk of inflammation, helping to regulate insulin levels and give you joint support. Sweet potatoes are part of the group of complex carbs sources as well as help to restore glycogen levels, which decrease significantly after a workout.

Post: Chocolate Milk
Proven by recent research, chocolate mild is an amazing post-workout snacks, even over water and sports drinks! It has everything you need: carbs and protein for muscle recovery, water content to replace the fluids you lost during a sweaty session, and calcium, sodium and sugar. All this ingredients help you recover faster, retain water and regain energy. Go get your choco milk!

Post: Veggie Omelet with Avocado
We already know that eggs are a great source of protein and help your muscle to recover and grow. Spice it up a little! Add a few slices of avocado for fiber and healthy fats. Like olive oil, avocados can help your body better absorb fat soluble nutrients that your veggies have like vitamins A, D, E and K. These vitamins are full with antioxidants, the best energy boost for your body!

Post: Grilled Chicken and Mixed Vegetables
Fuel your body with some nutritious goodness after a workout. The chicken is full of lean protein and carbohydrates that will fill you up, without giving you a bloaty stomach! Add some fresh vegetables and your body will be super happy!
